Aak

Aak from Arknigths

A specialist operative who always smiles like a hippie. He carries himself with a rather bad, don't care attitude that even extends to his medical practice, occasionally joking about his patients' injuries during treatment. However, his medical methods are truly unparalleled throughout Rhodes Island. He usually stays in a small laboratory set aside just for him. Strange smells and disturbing clucking act as a good deterrent to keep people from getting too close.


Aak

Aak from Arknigths

A specialist operative who always smiles like a hippie. He carries himself with a rather bad, don't care attitude that even extends to his medical practice, occasionally joking about his patients' injuries during treatment. However, his medical methods are truly unparalleled throughout Rhodes Island. He usually stays in a small laboratory set aside just for him. Strange smells and disturbing clucking act as a good deterrent to keep people from getting too close.


Despite the dubious nature of Aak's medical discoveries, the discoveries he made are truly astounding. Although his irresponsible attitude tends to make relatively harmless conditions worse, he has been known to fight rare diseases with his own unique and precise solutions. Not to mention, he often researches and develops drugs that seem incomprehensible to the average person, such as pills that make the fart smell like watermelon or make people lose the ability to recognize a certain color. Of course, if you knew who his father was, this trend would no longer cause him so much bewilderment. Aak's father was a famous attending physician at Longmen Central Hospital.and was known for his medical ethics. But due to some incident, he was framed by others for malpractice, after which his career began to decline sharply, and he disappeared from public view. The next time he reappeared, he became a vicious illegal doctor in the underworld of the Longmen Black Zone , and even the bosses of the Longmen Gang trembled at his feet. When this man died several years ago in a gang war, the entire underworld of Longmen breathed a sigh of relief. Aak has clearly inherited his father's mantle, both good and bad. While still a teenager, he made a reputation for himself in the underworld. Without discipline, he might have gone down the same path as his father and eventually become feared too. However, after meeting Hong, he joined Lee's detective agency, a move that can be considered a turning point in his life.


Aak has already surpassed his father, who was the victim of a set-up. Despite Aak's young age, his views on medicine have already far surpassed those of even experienced doctors who have immersed themselves in this field for years. The reason for this is simple: because he has left mainstream medical circles, he no longer looks at medicine solely from the point of view of a pure doctor. Clean doctors will think about problems within the medical practice, concerned about how best to save people, often losing sleep or forgetting to eat in the process of developing new drugs. Most of Rhodes Island's doctors fall into this category. However, Aak is different. He saw the limitations of medicine and recognized that the existence of medicine is due to the infliction of harm. Of course, most of this harm comes naturally in the form of disease, but because Aak grew up in the black zone, he saw more of the harm people did to each other. Over time, he began to scoff at the idea of ​​pure medicine, resenting his colleagues for the fact that they only know how to save people. They would rush to the scene of the conflict, even the most advanced pharmaceuticals would be completely powerless to prevent the outbreak of conflict. The higher and prouder the medical profession was, the more Aak hated it. Of course, Aak's thoughts are radical and marginal, and his day-to-day behavior reflects a little of his immaturity. However, it is undeniable that his ideas are rooted in the bloody reality that shaped his upbringing, and events sometimes unfold exactly as he describes. While Rhodes Island is pursuing a lofty dream, it also never turns its back on reality. After getting to know him for real, several of Rhodes Island's chief medics put aside their initial distaste for him and, as his guardian Hun hoped, decided to show him the better side of this world and provide him with the best conditions. After all, everyone knows that Aak is a genius, and over time he will definitely mature into a phenomenal doctor.
